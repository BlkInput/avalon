The Resistance: Avalon

A game of hidden roles, deduction, and deception

New Player Guide

What Is This Game?

Avalon is a social deduction game for 5 to 10 players. The kingdom is at war. A group of loyal knights must complete quests to save Camelot — but hidden among them are spies and traitors working to make those quests fail.

The catch: nobody knows who anyone else is. You'll have to watch how people vote, who they send on missions, and what they say — to figure out who you can trust.

Two Teams, One Winner

The Light

Good

Knights of Camelot. Your goal is to successfully complete 3 quests. You don't know who the evil players are — you have to figure it out through observation and debate.

Majority of players
The Shadow

Evil

Spies and traitors. Your goal is to make 3 quests fail — or trick the Assassin into killing Merlin at the end. Evil players know who each other are (with some exceptions).

Minority of players

How a Round Works

1
The Leader Proposes a Team

One player is the Leader each round. They choose a small group of players to go on the quest — the number required changes based on which quest it is and how many people are playing.

The leader changes every round, moving clockwise around the table.
2
Everyone Votes on the Team

All players simultaneously vote Approve or Reject. The votes are revealed at the same time. If more than half approve, the team goes on the quest. If rejected, the next player becomes leader and proposes a new team.

If 5 teams in a row are rejected, Evil wins automatically. Keep that in mind before you reject.
3
The Quest Team Plays Cards

Each player on the approved team secretly plays a Success or Fail card. Good players must play Success. Evil players can choose to play Fail. The cards are shuffled and revealed — but nobody knows who played what.

Even one Fail card is usually enough to sink a quest. Some quests require two Fails on larger player counts.
4
Repeat Until Someone Wins

Play continues through up to 5 quests. The first team to win 3 quests takes the match — unless Good wins 3 and then the Assassin guesses Merlin correctly.

How to Win

🏆

Good Wins If...

3 quests succeed.

But then — the Assassin gets one final attempt to identify and kill Merlin. If they guess correctly, Evil steals the victory.

💀

Evil Wins If...

3 quests fail.

Or 5 consecutive team proposals are rejected.

Or Good wins 3 quests but the Assassin correctly identifies Merlin.

The Roles

Good Roles
Merlin

Knows who all the evil players are — but must keep it secret. If Good wins, the Assassin will try to find and kill you. Act too obviously and you hand Evil the game.

Percival

Sees two players who could be Merlin or Morgana — but can't tell which is which. Your job is to protect Merlin while confusing Evil.

Loyal Servant of Arthur

No special information. Pay attention to who proposes what, who votes how, and who seems to know too much — or too little.

Evil Roles
Assassin

If Good wins 3 quests, you get one shot to name Merlin. Watch carefully — Merlin often subtly steers Good without revealing themselves.

Morgana

You appear as a possible Merlin to Percival. Use this to confuse and misdirect the good team.

Mordred

Merlin cannot see you as evil. You are invisible to the one person who knows everything — use that freedom wisely.

Oberon

You are evil — but you don't know who your teammates are, and they don't know you. You're on your own.

Minion of Mordred

Standard evil. You know your teammates and can coordinate — but Merlin can see you. Don't make it too obvious.

Tips for New Players

👁️
Watch who proposes who

Evil players will try to sneak each other onto quest teams. If someone keeps pushing for the same people, ask yourself why.

🗳️
Votes reveal information

After every vote, you know who approved and who rejected. Evil players will often approve teams that contain another evil player.

🎭
If you're Merlin, be subtle

You know everything — but if you're too obvious about steering the team, the Assassin will notice. Lose on purpose early if it protects your identity.

🤫
Evil should blend in early

Don't fail quests right away — building trust and then betraying it later is far more effective than being obvious from the start.

⚠️
Don't forget the 5-reject rule

If 5 team proposals are rejected in a row, Evil wins automatically. Sometimes Evil will keep rejecting just to trigger this — don't let them bait you into a deadlock.

🗡️
Good isn't safe until the very end

Even if Good wins 3 quests, the Assassin still gets a shot at Merlin. Winning the quests is only half the battle.
